Embedding hard choices into a game is one of the most entertaining things to watch. Marius, a cultist apparent, is suddenly trying to lead the party to help villagers at the sanctuary. As Marius tells the party that he used to live in Greenest and returned at the beginning of the attack, he was able to get himself one of the robes and used it to blend in with the assailants. While the party seemed to accept this answer, the players couldn’t help but think that there was more. Most players in the game would think that because “why else would the DM introduce this character?”

One of the things I had to do at this point was to ensure that Marius would do everything in his power to convince the party that he was not a cultist. In fact, Marius was not a cultist. He wants to defend his hometown, though he appears out of nowhere and suddenly has a robe. The party wants to ask more questions, but I urge the party with the urgency of the town’s destruction. With everything that had happened thus far, this wasn’t too farfetched.

To the northeast of where the party was, there is a wood that separates the Old Tunnel from the sanctuary. I didn’t place any monsters in this wood. One of the reasons for this is that there wouldn’t be any reason to put them there. Villagers would have fled for the apparent safety of the sanctuary and all of the cultists would be looking for loot. The wood, therefore, was a good staging area for the party to scope out the situation.

The party asked some probing questions of Marius, but Marius was able to tell the truth about his situation. However, the party was hesitant when Marius decided to stay back from the sanctuary battle. I didn’t want to give the players too much guidance or help because I wanted them to solve the situation themselves.

At the sanctuary, there are three problems. There is a band of strong cultists led by a dragonclaw at the front of the sanctuary, a band of raucus enemies in large numbers circling the sanctuary, and a few cultists trying to smoke the sanctuary out at the backdoor. From the vantage of the party, they had a clear view of the back and I counted seconds as the party deliberated. As the circling cultists may their way around the sanctuary and came into view (thanks again to dynamic lighting), their plans suddenly changed.

Now, for the fun part. Nysvyre originally just wanted to charge in and attack. This was strongly discouraged by the party, but wasn’t off the table. That is, until the other cultists arrived. Instead, the staunchly anti-cultist Nysvyre decided that he was going to put on a robe and pose as a cultist. They had a few robes that they began stripping off of other cultists. He donned one of the robes and Amaranth decided that he would use wild shape and jump in his pocket to listen in and help if need be. I made the courtyard big enough so that if there was an engagement, the other parties would have disadvantage on their attacks if they had to jump in for at least one turn.

Nysvyre strode forward to the cultists instructing the kobolds. The kobolds periodically would emerge from the smoke in order to grab more wood. The door wasn’t catching using what they were, but it sure did generate a lot of smoke. When Nysvyre arrive, another man in a red robe was giving the orders. Upon seeing Nysvyre was grateful that he had some assistance that wasn’t more kobolds. He told the cultist that they should go get actual wood, rather than whatever it is they were using that wasn’t working. Persuasion check fail. The man became agitated. “Who do you think you are? Why are you trying to order me around?” The man places a hand on his dagger. Nysvyre intimidation check success.

With this, one of two cultists takes a number of kobolds to go dismantle a nearby cart. However, that left two kobolds and a cultist remaining. Given a signal, the rest of the party rushed forward. The cultists, including Nysvyre, prepared to attack the incoming defenders. Kobold slings fired wildly, not finding their target. Crossbows and cantrips reciprocated, but to no avail. However, Nysvyre managed to cut down one kobold before the cultist realized what was happening. With everything happening so fast, the smoke obscuring the battlefield, and the inevitable arrival of the circling brigade, the party had to kill quickly and not take names. Two kobolds and a cultist fell without much trouble. Amaranth didn’t even change out of his mouse form.

They moved into the smoke for cover and attempted to try to enter the sanctuary. Obviously it was locked from the inside. No way to pick this. However, conveniently, Amaranth was in mouse form and crept through some broken parts of the door. He squeaked by and entered into the sanctuary. A thunderous roar resounded through the hall. The battering ram at the front had already started. There wasn’t much time.

Amaranth’s first priority was the door. He transformed into himself again, a wooden golem, and shocked everyone in the hall. The priest, a seasoned fighter, was ready to do battle with his longsword. “Who are you,” he demanded as Amaranth tried to persuade him that he was friendly. Persuasion check fail. He raises his sword to begin his attack. Amaranth, quickly thinking on his feet, refused to attack. With this, the priest lowered his guard. Persuasion with advantage success!

After convincing the priest that he was a friend, the villagers rushed toward the backdoor. Lifting the heavy bar that effectively locked the door, they pushed open the door. This left me with some quick explaining to do about how cathedral doors work. The backdoors actually open outward. Thus, when the thronging crowd pushed the door open, the party members that were pressed up to the other side were quickly pushed against the wall. The circling troupe was just passing the northeast corner of the sanctuary and the other cultist and kobolds were returning to the sanctuary. Seeing their comrades fallen, they rushed. Nysvyre and party sprang to attack. Amaranth told the people to run to the forest. Here's were pandemonium occurred.

All of the people began to rush toward the forest. Nysvyre charged the attacking cultist and kobolds. As he became exposed around the corner, seeing upwards of twenty enemies, he should have began thinking that perhaps this was a bad idea. He attacked anyway. Whack! Kobold down. The rest of the party tried to help, but kept their distance. At this time, I think dynamic lighting probably played a significant role because they may not have been aware of what was happening. Amelrelle threw daggers, Cora cast her cantrips, Ijaia shot her crossbow. Not all of the enemies fell.

A hail of rocks and arrows fell upon Nysvyre. Pack tactics proved to be a nightmare as Nysvyre fell unconscious. The party was faced with a difficult dilemma. The party could easily be wiped if they tried to help Nysvyre. It might take multiple turns to even get him moving again. The party didn’t have much spells because they hadn’t gotten a long rest. Should they carry him? Should they leave him? They ended up casting healing word and Nysvyre rose up. Luckily, during that time, they were able to slay the enemies to avoid opportunity attacks and the other enemies were too far away to engage in melee that turn. With disadvantage, they were unable to succeed against Nysvyre’s higher armor class. However, I think I did, as a DM, decide that one of the hits did not actually hit. If it had, the party would have likely lost Nysvyre or died trying to save him.

The turn that Nysvyre stood up, the door to the sanctuary was broken open. The enemies at the front charged through and now there were too many problems. The party fled for the forest. Marius helped the villagers take cover and keep safe. The party made it to the forest edge and noted that a half-dragon told the cultist’s to stop their attack (noting that he held up his clenched fist). The party seemed to have survived.

Making their way into the woods, there was a general feeling of despair in the air. The sanctuary of Chantea was just seized and the villagers had just escaped with their lives. While in the book Greenest is described as a hamlet, I find it hard to believe that a hamlet would have a keep at the center of town. Therefore, I increased the population to nearly 10,000. While that isn’t extraordinary, it means that the number of people held up in the sanctuary would be sufficient to be troublesome. Men, women, and children desperately clung to one another as they panicked. During the flight, some of the slower ones could have been trampled had the party not done their part to assist.

In the woods, many of the villagers could see the fires that the cultists had started. For all they knew, Greenest was lost and they had managed to be saved at the last minute. Chantea be praised. However, the party was in the wood with a loud commotion that they needed to get to the keep. Rather than walking into the front door, which would be dangerous. But first, they needed to calm everyone down. Nysvyre tried to tell everyone to be quiet, but failed his intimidation check. Amaranth tried to calm everyone down, but failed his persuasion check. Cora attempted to calm everyone down, but also failed her persuasion check. With so many failed checks going on, tempers were flaring. No one had tried to talk to the priest to get him to calm everyone down.

Ijaia pinged me on Discord to say that she wanted to try to play a song. This is one of the nice things about playing online is that people can slip you messages and not worry about talking over people (which happens more online I imagine anyway). I had her roll a performance check. Natural 20! As everyone was chatting, I changed the music in the game to something soft and melodious as Ijaia began playing her panflute. I elaborated on the eloquence and the soothing tones that seemed to weave with the wind and the trees. Somber, yes, but seemed to be a ballad of faith and change. The priest of the sanctuary approached and thanked her for her performance. Calm had been brought to the wood.

Marius was revealed to be talking to his old friend from the sanctuary, who then vouched for Marius. They discussed that they fled to the sanctuary when the cult first attacked and many of the parishioners were afraid to leave. Before they knew it, they were trapped on all sides. After praising governor Nighthill for sending help (to which Cora rolled her eyes), they told them that they would return to the keep through the Old Tunnel. Not even the priest knew of this entrance, but it seemed like the right way to go.

This session was largely centered around the sanctuary. It wasn’t a long session. Not many things were completed. However, the party had a lot of engagement with NPCs and enemies. There were a couple of small battles and the party avoided an extremely large battle that would have gone poorly for them. I enjoyed the session and many of the players were relieved that they didn’t die. Many weren’t too happy with Nysvyre charging into battle so carelessly, though in the end things worked out. People take time to make decisions. This was one of the elements that the book pressed on, though it took care of itself. I thought the players were creative, though I definitely didn’t do justice to the amount of dialogue that the players had when trying to make a decision about what to do. It also took Amaranth some time to remember that he was a mouse and could crawl under the door into the sanctuary. But, they succeeded.
